Now you may be thinking;
"Why do I need to buy a proper violin? Can't I just rip some hair off a horse and start rubbing it against a tree branch? Isn't it basically the same thing?"
And the short answer is yes, you can. But then the violin companies go out of business and that's just not very nice, is it? So just be cool, buy a proper one, and everybody can be happy.

At the bottom of the page are included some useful links to good online violin shops, but the best way to buy a violin is at your local music shop. Buying a violin in person lets you test the weight, sound quality, and general feel of the instrument, something that just isn't possible online. However if, like me, a crippling sense of social anxiety prevents you from even looking out the window without breaking into a cold sweat, the links below may be helpful to you.
